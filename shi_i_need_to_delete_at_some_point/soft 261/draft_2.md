# SOFT 261 - Take Home Final (Revised Feedback)

## Presentation 1 - Mutual of Omaha

### 1. Visual Communication

#### What went well

1. The use of JSON and relational database diagrams clearly illustrated how data is structured and connected. This helps audience members who may not have prior knowledge understand the system architecture.

2. The GUI screenshots effectively showed how users would interact with the system. This made the product feel concrete and helped communicate its practical use within the company.

3. The presentation consistently attempted to visualize abstract technical concepts, which is important for communicating complex systems to an audience of employers as well as students and staff of UNL.

#### What needs improvement

1. The color palette used in the product solution slides was inconsistent with the rest of the presentation. This reduced readability, especially in a bright room. Using a consistent color scheme across all slides would improve visual cohesion and clarity.

2. Some diagrams relied on technical terminology like frameworks without sufficient visual explanation. This made them difficult for non-technical audience members to understand. Simplifying the diagram or adding analogies or labels would improve accessibility.

3. Several slides contained dense information without clear visual hierarchy. This made it difficult to identify the key message. Emphasizing key points through size, spacing, or color would improve clarity.

### 2. Delivery

#### What went well

1. The speakers demonstrated strong technical understanding and explained complex ideas clearly. This helped the audience follow the purpose and functionality of the system.

2. The presenters effectively responded to audience questions by providing both direct answers and additional relevant context, which improved overall understanding.

3. The introduction of the company and project context was clear and helped orient the audience early in the presentation.

#### What needs improvement

4. The explanation of stakeholders was rushed, making it difficult to understand each role. Slowing down and briefly explaining each role’s responsibilities would improve clarity.

5. The transition to the award segment disrupted the flow of the presentation. Providing a clearer transition or briefly signaling the shift in topic would make the presentation feel more cohesive.

6. Some sections were delivered quickly, which reduced comprehension of important details. More consistent pacing would improve audience understanding.

## Presentation 2 - Career Explore XR

### 1. Visual Communication

#### What went well

1. Stakeholders were clearly separated into distinct slides, which helped organize the information and made each group easier to understand.

2. Role titles were included with each stakeholder, which clearly communicated responsibilities and relationships within the project.

3. The overall slide structure followed a logical progression, helping the audience follow the narrative of the project.

#### What needs improvement

4. Some slides relied heavily on text without supporting visuals. This made it harder to quickly understand the key message. Adding diagrams or images would improve engagement and clarity.

5. Screenshots were not labeled, which made it difficult for viewers to remember what each image represented. Adding clear labels or annotations would improve comprehension.

6. The problem and opportunity slide could be simplified and visually emphasized. Reducing text and highlighting key points would make the message clearer.

### 2. Delivery

#### What went well

1. The project’s goal was clearly introduced at the beginning, which helped establish context for the rest of the presentation.

2. The explanation of the problem (lack of skilled trade workers) was simple and easy to understand, making the motivation for the project clear.

3. The speakers maintained a logical flow throughout the presentation, which supported audience comprehension.

#### What needs improvement

1. Some explanations were broader than necessary, which made parts of the presentation feel unfocused. Focusing on a specific use case would improve clarity dramatically.

2. The inclusion of background information about UNL’s School of Computing was unnecessary for this audience and did not contribute to the main message because in order to attend this event you would have to know about it beforehand. Removing or shortening this section would improve focus.

3. Certain transitions between topics were abrupt. Adding brief transition statements would improve flow and coherence.
