Mutal of Omaha
What works well
Speaker technical presentation skills
Both speakers in this presentation showcased the technical aspects of their project very well verbally. This is quite impressive because that’s very difficult to do. I learned quite quickly what a database schema meant. When asked a question they not only gave the best relevant information but also presented additional information that was useful. To be more precise, one audience member asked if their project was flexible with other databases other than the one to do with the Mutual of Omaha bank, and the 1st speaker said that due to the schema of the database being universally used, the project was very flexible and that it was a relational database. This was useful because I did not know how this project operated, now I do.
Speaker company contextualization skills
The 1st speaker of this project very quickly and effectively introduced the audience into what the Mutual of Omaha bank was. This was useful as I and seemingly other audience members did not know what the Mutal of Omaha was
Json file and relational diagrams
These are extremely important and effectively useful for anybody who doesn’t know what a Json file is or what a relational database it. The diagrams they have for these are incredible.

What needs improvement
Stakeholder contextualization
The stakeholders of this project were covered too fast. I couldn’t understand who was the sponsors were or what roles the UNL school of computing faculty were doing. To improve this the speaker had to slow down. That and the speaker had to create a better verbal explanation of who the stakeholders were and what they did.
Product solution diagram
Thier 2 product solution diagrams had white text on a black background when the rest of their slideshow had black text on a white background. While viewing this is easy in a dark environment, this slideshow was veiwed in a very well lit room, which makes viewing it difficult.
Reading the diagram as intended is difficult as many of the terms used in the diagram are not well known among non-devloper audience members. What is spring boot API? What is Amazon Bedrock? wha tis Amazon SES? Background worker? everything else. I don’t know what those are doing, or how they’re interacting with each other. It looks like these components are going one way all the way down to MongoDB and PostgreSQL, and again. I don’t know what those are.
The way I would fix this would be to present the presentation in terms of a user instead of a developer. I say this because it makes everything in the slideshow’s message much more understandable.
